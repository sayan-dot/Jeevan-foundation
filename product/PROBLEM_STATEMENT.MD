**Last Updated:** 30 June 2026  
**Status:** Active

# Purpose

This document defines the core problems Jeevan exists to solve.

Every feature developed for Jeevan must directly address one or more of the problems described in this document.

If a feature does not solve a meaningful problem, it should not be built.

---

# The Problem

Healthcare has become increasingly digital.

Unfortunately, many healthcare applications have become more complicated rather than more helpful.

People are expected to navigate unfamiliar interfaces, remember important information, understand medical terminology, and manage their healthcare independently.

For millions of people, especially elderly users, this creates unnecessary stress and confusion.

Healthcare should reduce anxiety.

Technology should not increase it.

---

# Primary Problems

## 1. People Forget Important Medicines

Many individuals miss medication because they:

- Forget the time
- Lose track of schedules
- Become distracted
- Cannot manage multiple medicines

Missing medication can reduce treatment effectiveness and, in some cases, become life-threatening.

---

## 2. Elderly Users Struggle with Technology

Many healthcare applications assume users are comfortable with smartphones.

In reality, many elderly users struggle with:

- Small text
- Complex navigation
- Technical terminology
- Too many options
- Information overload

Technology should adapt to users—not expect users to adapt to technology.

---

## 3. Families Constantly Worry

Family members often ask questions such as:

- Did my parents take today's medicine?
- Did they miss a reminder?
- Are they managing their health properly?

Current solutions rarely provide reassurance without requiring frequent phone calls or manual follow-ups.

---

## 4. Healthcare Information is Fragmented

Important healthcare information is often spread across:

- Paper prescriptions
- Medicine strips
- Hospital files
- Reminder applications
- Notes
- Calendars

Managing this information becomes increasingly difficult over time.

---

## 5. Existing Healthcare Applications Feel Overwhelming

Many healthcare applications attempt to solve every problem at once.

As a result, they become difficult to learn and stressful to use.

More features do not always create a better experience.

Simplicity creates confidence.

---

# Our Opportunity

Jeevan has the opportunity to rethink healthcare technology from the perspective of people rather than software.

Instead of asking:

"What features should we build?"

We ask:

"What problems should we remove?"

This philosophy shapes every product decision.

---

# Phase 1 Focus

The first version of Jeevan focuses on one problem.

Helping users remember and manage their medicines.

Nothing else should distract from this objective.

Future phases will expand only after this experience becomes reliable, simple, and trustworthy.

---

# Long-Term Problems We Intend to Solve

As Jeevan grows, the platform aims to address additional healthcare challenges, including:

- Appointment management
- Caregiver support
- Emergency assistance
- Medical record organization
- Health monitoring
- Healthcare accessibility
- Healthcare education
- Intelligent health guidance

Each new capability must solve a clearly identified problem.

---

# Success Criteria

A problem is considered solved only when users can complete their healthcare tasks:

- With less effort
- With less confusion
- With greater confidence
- With greater independence

Technology should become almost invisible.

The healthcare experience should become simpler.

---

# Guiding Question

Before building any feature, ask:

**"Which problem does this solve?"**

If no clear answer exists, the feature should not be built.

---

> "People don't need more healthcare features. They need fewer healthcare problems."
