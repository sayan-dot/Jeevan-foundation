# Sentinel

> "Observing quietly. Acting only when confidence is high."

---

# What is Sentinel?

Sentinel is Jeevan's intelligent safety engine.

It continuously learns a user's normal routine and detects significant deviations using multiple independent sources of information.

Sentinel does not diagnose diseases.

Sentinel detects unusual situations that may require attention.

---

# Mission

Provide early awareness during potentially dangerous situations while respecting user privacy and avoiding unnecessary panic.

---

# Philosophy

Every person has unique habits.

Sentinel never compares one user to another.

Sentinel learns one person.

Every decision is personalized.

---

# Core Principles

No single signal is trusted.

No single event triggers an emergency.

Confidence is built from independent evidence.

Privacy comes before intelligence.

False alarms are better reduced than increased.

User safety always comes first.

# Escalation Levels

Level 0

Everything Normal

No action.

---

Level 1

Small anomaly.

Continue observing.

Collect more evidence.

No interruption.

---

Level 2

Moderate confidence.

Sarathi gently checks in.

Example

"Rajiv, I noticed something unusual."

"If you're okay, tap the screen or say 'I'm okay.'"

Listen for response.

Wait.

Observe.

---

Level 3

High confidence.

Request stronger confirmation.

Example

"If you need help, make any loud sound or call for help."

Continue listening.

Continue monitoring movement.

Continue monitoring activity.

---

Level 4

Very High Confidence

User is not responding.

Notify emergency contacts.

Share last known location.

Continue collecting data.

---

Level 5

Critical Situation

Multiple independent signals strongly indicate a dangerous situation.

Emergency workflow begins according to regional settings and user preferences.

# Success

The goal of Sentinel is not to detect emergencies.

The goal is to recognize that something is genuinely unusual with enough confidence to act appropriately.

Sentinel should remain invisible 99.9% of the time.

The best Sentinel is the one users never notice until the day they truly need it.
